What is mental security? (personal point of view)
   Mental security is the human right of having a personal secured mental space for his/her own. It includes privacy of thoughts, feelings, choices, preferences, health issues. Also, it includes the right of protecting and sharing or not, any personal aspect, without the fear of criticism or judgement.
Ethical issues
   The intersection of mental security and AI raises some ethical issues:

  • Privacy and confidentiality: the tools created by AI may collect sensitive personal data.

  • Individual's consent: should be given before collecting, analyze and share personal information.

  • Transparency and accountability of AI systems

  • Dependency & possible manipulation of emotions, behaviors or perception: related to the psychological factor, one of the biggest problem that could arise is the dependency AI tools can create, in the detriment of human interaction. Moreover, this dependency could include manipulation of emotions, behaviors or perception.

Has my perception of mental privacy changed over time?
   Back in the times, in my home country, it was a normal thing to know personal information about your friends and neighbors, like their salary or health issues. Because of the legal changes, life experiences or societal shifts, now the situation is totally different. No one is so eager anymore to reveal details about their own or about others: pictures are not so easily posted on social media, thoughts are shared only with very trustful friends and some topics are taboo (like earnings, religion, family issues).
    So, yes, changes occur during the years either on a personal level, either on the societal level. These changes may include positive aspects: awareness and proactive measures to protect privacy, empowerment of the self, better relationship dynamics, legal protection. On the other hand, being too aware of the personal protection leads in some cases to anxiety, social isolation, worse relationships, reluctance to seek support from others.
